# General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) — EU Regulation 2016/679

## What it is
The EU's comprehensive data protection regulation with extraterritorial reach, governing the processing of personal data of EU residents. Sets the global gold standard for privacy regulation, influencing India's DPDP Act and similar laws worldwide.

## Who it applies to
Any organization processing personal data of individuals in the European Economic Area (EEA), regardless of where the organization is located. Applies to Indian companies serving EU customers.

## Key obligations
- Establish a lawful basis for processing personal data (consent, contract, legitimate interest, etc.)
- Implement Privacy by Design and Privacy by Default
- Appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO) where required
- Conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) for high-risk processing
- Honour data subject rights: access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, objection
- Report personal data breaches to the supervisory authority within 72 hours
- Maintain Records of Processing Activities (ROPA)
- Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
- Ensure lawful cross-border data transfers (SCCs, adequacy decisions, BCRs)
- Manage data processors through GDPR-compliant Data Processing Agreements

## Maximum penalty
Up to €20 million or 4% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher

## Enforcement deadline
In force since May 25, 2018
