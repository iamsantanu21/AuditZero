# Information Technology Act, 2000

## What it is
India's foundational cyber law that provides legal recognition for electronic transactions, digital signatures, and defines cybercrimes and penalties. It also establishes the adjudicatory framework for cyber disputes.

## Who it applies to
All persons, companies, and intermediaries using or providing electronic/digital services in India.

## Key obligations
- Maintain reasonable security practices and procedures (Section 43A)
- Comply with intermediary due diligence obligations (Section 79)
- Report cyber incidents to CERT-In as mandated
- Implement digital signature/electronic signature infrastructure where required
- Preserve and retain traffic data and user information as directed by government
- Block content as directed by government orders under Section 69A
- Follow data protection rules under Section 43A for sensitive personal data
- Appoint Grievance Officers for intermediary platforms
- Cooperate with law enforcement in cybercrime investigations
- Maintain audit trails for electronic records

## Maximum penalty
Up to ₹5 crore compensation + imprisonment up to life for certain offences (Sections 66-74)

## Enforcement deadline
In force since October 17, 2000
