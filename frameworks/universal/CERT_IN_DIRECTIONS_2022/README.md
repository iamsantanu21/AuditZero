# CERT-In Directions on Information Security Practices, 2022

## What it is
Mandatory directions requiring all organizations to report cyber incidents to CERT-In within 6 hours, synchronize system clocks with NTP, maintain logs for 180 days, and follow prescribed security practices for VPN providers, cloud services, and virtual asset service providers.

## Who it applies to
All service providers, intermediaries, data centres, body corporates, and government organizations in India.

## Key obligations
- Report cyber security incidents to CERT-In within 6 hours of discovery
- Synchronize ICT system clocks to NTP server of NIC or NPL (or equivalent)
- Maintain logs of all ICT systems for a rolling period of 180 days within India
- Designate a Point of Contact (PoC) with CERT-In
- VPN providers must maintain subscriber logs for 5 years
- Virtual asset service providers must maintain KYC and transaction records for 5 years
- Cloud service providers must maintain customer registration data for 5 years
- Data centres must maintain customer information for 5 years
- Enable and preserve audit logs of all critical systems
- Take action or provide information/assistance to CERT-In within timeframes specified

## Maximum penalty
Up to ₹1 lakh per non-compliance + additional penalties under IT Act 2000

## Enforcement deadline
Effective from June 28, 2022
