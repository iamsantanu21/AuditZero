# Information Technology (Intermediary Guidelines) Rules, 2011

## What it is
Rules governing the due diligence obligations of internet intermediaries under the IT Act. These have been substantially superseded by the IT (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021 for most intermediary obligations.

## Who it applies to
Internet intermediaries including social media platforms, e-commerce marketplaces, search engines, ISPs, and cloud service providers.

## Key obligations
- Publish terms of service/user agreement prohibiting specified content
- Inform users about privacy policy and terms of use
- Remove/disable access to unlawful content within 36 hours of court order or government notification
- Preserve information for 90 days for investigation purposes when directed
- Appoint a Grievance Officer and publish contact details
- Respond to user complaints within one month
- Not knowingly host or publish prohibited content
- Cooperate with law enforcement agencies
- Implement due diligence as prescribed to retain safe harbour protection

## Maximum penalty
Loss of intermediary safe harbour protection under Section 79 of IT Act

## Enforcement deadline
In force since April 11, 2011 (partially superseded by 2021 Rules)
