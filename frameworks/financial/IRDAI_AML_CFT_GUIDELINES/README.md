# IRDAI Master Guidelines on Anti-Money Laundering, 2022

## What it is
IRDAI's consolidated AML/CFT guidelines for insurance companies mandating customer due diligence, risk categorization, suspicious transaction reporting, and record keeping to prevent money laundering through insurance products.

## Who it applies to
All insurance companies (life and general) and insurance intermediaries.

## Key obligations
- Implement Customer Due Diligence (CDD) procedures for all policyholders
- Conduct Enhanced Due Diligence for high-risk customers and PEPs
- File Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) with FIU-IND within prescribed timelines
- Maintain customer identification and transaction records for at least 5 years
- Appoint a Principal Officer for AML/CFT compliance
- Implement risk-based categorization of customers (low, medium, high)
- Screen customers against UNSC sanctions lists and domestic watch lists
- Conduct AML/CFT training for staff and agents
- Conduct independent AML/CFT audits periodically
- Monitor high-value transactions and cash payments in insurance premiums

## Maximum penalty
Up to ₹1 crore + regulatory action under Insurance Act and PMLA

## Enforcement deadline
In force since 2022
