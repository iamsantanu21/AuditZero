# RBI Digital Lending Directions, 2025

## What it is
Updated framework governing digital lending through apps and platforms, mandating that all loan disbursements and repayments flow directly between the borrower and the regulated entity's bank account, with strict norms on data privacy, transparency, and lending service provider conduct.

## Who it applies to
Banks, NBFCs, and their Lending Service Providers (LSPs) and Digital Lending Apps (DLAs).

## Key obligations
- All loan disbursements must be made directly to borrower's bank account
- No automatic increase in credit limit without explicit consent
- Disclose Key Fact Statement (KFS) including APR before loan execution
- LSPs/DLAs must not store personal data beyond what is needed
- Implement robust grievance redressal mechanism with escalation to RBI Ombudsman
- Publish list of LSPs/DLAs engaged on the regulated entity's website
- Cooling-off/look-up period for borrowers to exit loans without penalty
- Data collected by DLAs must be need-based with prior consent
- No access to mobile phone contacts, media, or call logs
- Report all digital lending activities in regulatory returns

## Maximum penalty
Up to ₹50 crore + regulatory action including licence cancellation

## Enforcement deadline
In force; 2025 updates effective from notification date
