# RBI Master Direction — Know Your Customer (KYC) Direction, 2016

## What it is
Comprehensive KYC/AML directions requiring banks and regulated entities to verify customer identity, monitor transactions, maintain records, and report suspicious transactions. Forms the backbone of India's anti-money laundering compliance regime.

## Who it applies to
Banks, NBFCs, payment system providers, and other RBI-regulated entities.

## Key obligations
- Implement Customer Due Diligence (CDD) and Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) procedures
- Verify identity of customers using Aadhaar-based e-KYC, Video KYC, or document-based KYC
- Maintain customer identification records for at least 5 years after account closure
- File Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) with Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU-IND)
- Conduct periodic KYC updates (2 years for high-risk, 10 years for low-risk customers)
- Screen customers against UNSC sanctions lists and domestic watch lists
- Appoint a Principal Officer for KYC/AML compliance
- Implement risk-based approach to customer categorization
- Train staff on KYC/AML procedures
- Maintain records of all transactions above ₹10 lakh (cash) and ₹50 lakh (wire transfers)

## Maximum penalty
Up to ₹50 crore + directions for licence cancellation in extreme cases

## Enforcement deadline
In force since February 25, 2016 (continuously updated)
