# Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA), 2002 & AML/CFT Regulations

## What it is
India's primary anti-money laundering legislation and associated RBI circulars mandating customer due diligence, suspicious transaction reporting, and record keeping for all reporting entities in the financial sector.

## Who it applies to
Banks, financial institutions, NBFCs, payment system operators, insurance companies, securities intermediaries, and other reporting entities under PMLA.

## Key obligations
- Implement Customer Due Diligence (CDD) and Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)
- File Cash Transaction Reports (CTRs) for transactions above ₹10 lakh
- File Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) with FIU-IND within 7 days
- Maintain transaction records for at least 5 years from date of transaction
- Maintain customer identification records for at least 5 years after account closure
- Appoint a Principal Officer as designated compliance officer
- Train employees on AML/CFT obligations
- Screen customers against UNSC and India sanction lists
- Implement wire transfer rules for cross-border and domestic transfers
- Report counterfeit currency and cross-border transactions as prescribed

## Maximum penalty
Imprisonment 3-7 years + fine up to ₹5 lakh; attachment and confiscation of property

## Enforcement deadline
In force since July 1, 2005 (continuously updated)
