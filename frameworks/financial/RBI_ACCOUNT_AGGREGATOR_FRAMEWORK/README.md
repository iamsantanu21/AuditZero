# RBI Master Direction — Non-Banking Financial Company — Account Aggregator

## What it is
Framework enabling consent-based data sharing between financial information providers (banks, insurers, etc.) and financial information users through licensed Account Aggregators, forming the basis of India's Open Finance ecosystem.

## Who it applies to
Account Aggregators (NBFC-AA), Financial Information Providers (FIPs), and Financial Information Users (FIUs).

## Key obligations
- Obtain NBFC-AA licence from RBI before operating as Account Aggregator
- Obtain explicit, informed, revocable consent from customers before data sharing
- Do not store, process, or sell customer financial data — act only as a conduit
- Implement prescribed consent artifact format and data flow standards
- Maintain minimum net owned funds of ₹2 crore
- Comply with IT security and data protection standards prescribed by RBI
- Ensure interoperability with all registered FIPs and FIUs
- Submit periodic returns and reports to RBI
- Implement robust grievance redressal mechanism for customers
- Undergo annual audit by RBI-approved auditors

## Maximum penalty
Up to ₹50 crore + licence cancellation

## Enforcement deadline
In force since September 2, 2016 (operational from September 2021)
