# SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013

## What it is
Regulations governing registration, conduct, and obligations of investment advisers in India, including qualification requirements, client suitability assessments, fee structures, and conflict of interest management.

## Who it applies to
Any person or entity providing investment advice for consideration, including robo-advisors and digital investment advisory platforms.

## Key obligations
- Obtain SEBI registration before providing investment advice
- Meet prescribed qualification and experience requirements (NISM certification)
- Conduct client risk profiling and suitability assessment before providing advice
- Maintain clear separation between advisory and distribution activities
- Charge advisory fees only on AUM-based or fixed-fee basis (no commission from products)
- Maintain records of all advice given and client communications for 5 years
- Disclose all conflicts of interest to clients
- Implement robust KYC and client onboarding procedures
- Ensure compliance with AML/CFT obligations
- File periodic compliance reports with SEBI

## Maximum penalty
Up to ₹25 crore + suspension/cancellation of registration

## Enforcement deadline
In force since January 21, 2013 (latest amendments effective 2024)
