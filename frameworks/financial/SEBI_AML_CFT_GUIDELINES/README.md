# SEBI Guidelines on Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Standards and Combating Financing of Terrorism (CFT)

## What it is
SEBI's master circular consolidating AML/CFT guidelines for securities market intermediaries, covering client due diligence, suspicious transaction monitoring, record keeping, and reporting obligations under PMLA.

## Who it applies to
All SEBI-registered intermediaries including stock brokers, depository participants, mutual fund AMCs, portfolio managers, and investment advisers.

## Key obligations
- Implement Customer Due Diligence (CDD) at account opening and periodically thereafter
- File Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) with FIU-IND
- Maintain transaction and identification records for at least 5 years
- Appoint a designated Principal Officer for AML/CFT compliance
- Conduct Enhanced Due Diligence for high-risk clients and PEPs
- Screen clients against UNSC sanctions lists
- Implement risk-based categorization of clients
- Train employees on AML/CFT obligations
- Conduct independent AML/CFT audits
- Maintain records of wire transfers and cross-border transactions

## Maximum penalty
Up to ₹25 crore + regulatory action under SEBI Act and PMLA

## Enforcement deadline
In force (continuously updated via circulars)
