# NMC Guidelines for Practice of Telemedicine in India

## What it is
Guidelines enabling registered medical practitioners to provide healthcare services via telemedicine (video, audio, text consultations). Covers patient consent, prescribing norms, technology standards, and limitations of remote consultations.

## Who it applies to
Registered Medical Practitioners (RMPs) and telemedicine/healthtech platforms facilitating teleconsultations.

## Key obligations
- Only Registered Medical Practitioners (RMPs) may provide telemedicine consultations
- Obtain explicit patient consent before telemedicine consultation
- Verify patient identity before prescribing medication
- Follow prescribing restrictions (List A/B/C medications) based on consultation mode
- Maintain proper medical records of telemedicine consultations for 3 years
- Provide first consultation within 24 hours for emergency cases or refer to in-person care
- Use secure technology platforms ensuring patient data privacy
- Display RMP registration number and qualifications on the platform
- Follow up with patients as clinically required
- Refer to in-person consultation when telemedicine is insufficient

## Maximum penalty
Disciplinary action by NMC/State Medical Councils (suspension/cancellation of registration)

## Enforcement deadline
In force since March 25, 2020
