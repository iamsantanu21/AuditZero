# Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020

## What it is
Rules governing e-commerce entities in India, mandating transparency in pricing, product listings, seller information disclosure, grievance redressal, and prohibiting unfair trade practices on marketplace and inventory-based e-commerce platforms.

## Who it applies to
All e-commerce entities (marketplace and inventory models) selling goods or services to consumers in India.

## Key obligations
- Display seller/entity details including legal name, address, and customer care
- Provide clear product descriptions including country of origin
- Display total price including all taxes and charges (no hidden costs)
- Implement a grievance redressal mechanism with designated Grievance Officer
- Acknowledge complaints within 48 hours and resolve within 1 month
- Do not manipulate prices or post fake reviews
- Provide easy-to-understand cancellation and return policies
- Ensure marketplace neutrality — no preferential treatment to any seller
- Maintain records of sellers and their compliance with applicable laws
- Appoint Chief Compliance Officer and Nodal Contact Person

## Maximum penalty
Penalties under Consumer Protection Act, 2019 — up to ₹50 lakh for misleading ads; jurisdiction-specific amounts

## Enforcement deadline
In force since July 23, 2020
