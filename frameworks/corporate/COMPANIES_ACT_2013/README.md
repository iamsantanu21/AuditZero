# Companies Act, 2013

## What it is
India's primary corporate governance legislation governing incorporation, management, and dissolution of companies. It covers director duties, financial reporting, audit requirements, CSR obligations, and corporate governance standards.

## Who it applies to
All companies incorporated in India and foreign companies with business presence in India.

## Key obligations
- Maintain statutory registers and file annual returns with MCA
- Prepare and file audited financial statements annually
- Hold Board meetings at prescribed frequency (minimum 4 per year)
- Comply with Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) obligations if applicable
- Appoint independent directors and constitute Board committees as prescribed
- File event-based forms (allotment, charge creation, change in directors, etc.)
- Maintain proper books of accounts and internal financial controls
- Comply with related party transaction norms and disclosure requirements
- Report fraud to the Central Government within prescribed timelines
- Comply with winding up and dissolution procedures when required

## Maximum penalty
Up to ₹250 crore for companies + imprisonment for officers in default

## Enforcement deadline
In force since April 1, 2014 (continuous amendments)
